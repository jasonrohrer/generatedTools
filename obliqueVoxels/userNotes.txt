I'm wondering if we can have a "smoothers" tool, that behaves like a 1-thick line tool, and adds translucent cube smoothers to certain spots.  These smoothers are only visible when the smoothers tool is active (so it's not cluttering the view when we just want to see the voxels).  Smoothers are drawn as little translucent boxes.  Smoothers can't be placed inside existing voxels (if the line tool placing the smoother crosses a voxel along the way, that smoother is skipped), and if a voxel is drawn later that covers a smoother, the smoother disappears.  What the smoother does is take whatever faces it its touching, average those colors (or find a color between them on the ramp) and then sets ALL faces to that average (or middle ramp) color.  If there are only two neighboring colors on the ramp with nothing in between, it picks the brigther one.  In natural shading, it can always use the average color.  This should be visible in the Preview Match Render 3D view, and also in the resulting pixel art render.  So instead of strips of alternating color down the face of a sphere, we'd have a uniform patch of color in that are, thanks to the smoothers that we placed there.
