We already have right click to rotate, so left-clicking with the pencil tool shouldn't rotate too (redundant), and also, we should be able to just drag around and scribble with the pencil too, and add squares to any face hit.  Scribbling in the same spot, while looking down, should build a tower up.  Almost like the pencil tool is gobbing more and more bits of clay in whatever new spot (voxel face or floor face) that you mouse back over.  The other drawing tools allow dragging, but of course you drag a preview and let go to actually place the voxels.  The pencil tool should behave like the Scribble select tool or like the Smoother face tool, but instead of selecting voxels or picking faces to smooth, it should lay down blocks.  It might be too slow to update the pixel rendering as you drag around, so maybe you can drag a preview of little translucent blocks, and then when you let go, they appear all at once (just like the sphere or box).  If that's the case, then probably just add translucent preview blocks stuck only to the closest real (not preview) voxel face hit by the mouse.  In that case, piling up preview blocks on top of each other would be too confusing.  So the pencil drag would "paint" hit surfaces with new preview blocks, and then when you stop dragging, they become real voxels.
