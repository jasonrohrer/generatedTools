    File > Import PNG as voxel wall... loads a PNG (RGBA; pixels with alpha <
    128 are skipped) via stb_image and arms the new "Image wall" tool.  Three
    clicks place it: (1) the bottom-left corner cell, (2) the image-width axis,
    (3) a perpendicular image-height axis (commits).  The two axes snap to one
    of the six face directions by matching the cursor's on-screen direction
    away from the corner (screen-space/gizmo style), so you can aim a vertical
    +Y wall or a flat floor from any camera -- axis 2 is constrained
    perpendicular to axis 1.  Stage 1 previews a translucent column of
    image-width voxels; stage 2 previews the whole wall tinted with each
    pixel's nearest-palette colour.  Each opaque pixel becomes one flat
    single-colour voxel; the whole wall is one undo step.  Esc resets
    placement, and you can stamp another copy without reloading.
