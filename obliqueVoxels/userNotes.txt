--When the smoothed surface comes up against a non-smoothed surface, there is a boundary issue that we currently have no way to deal with.  The best case to demonstrate this is a cylinder.  We want the walls to be smooth, but the top to be flat, with a sharp edge between the top and the walls.  If we smooth the whole thing, we get shading that looks like a rounded edge (correct!)  But if we deselect just the very top layer, and smooth the walls only, then there's a weird spot in the shading along the top edge.  The top surface indeed looks flat, and the edge looks sharp, but where it meets the smoothing surface, it looks weird.  I wonder if there's some way to further specify a "corner" or something along with the smoothing surface... another button called "smooth to corner" that extends the neighboring smoothing surface up to a corner on the selected voxels.  So then there are three states voxels can be in:

1.  Unsmooth (regular voxels)
2.  Smooth   (what we have currently)
3.  Smooth Corner  (new)

I guess one thing about these smooth corner spots is that.... they behave like smooth voxels wherever they have smooth neighbors (including diagonal neighbors)... anywhere they are touching.  But if they stick out into the air on one side, or butt up against non-smooth voxels, they keep their sharp corner on that side.  In the case of the cylinder, the "top rim" would be a ring of these Smooth Corner voxels.  They would be smoothed down below, where they meet the smooth voxels on the cylinder walls, and they'd be smoothed around the circumference of the rim, where they touch the other Smooth Corner voxels around the rim, but on their top edge, where they hit the air, they would be sharp.


A cylinder isn't the only case here... imagine a half-sphere (dome)... we want the flat side to have a sharp edge that meets the smooth dome nicely.

This is also an issue if you want to punch a sharp-edged hole through a smoothed surface, like punching a square hole through the front wall of a smooth cylinder.  I'd want to make the rim of the hole Smooth Corners
